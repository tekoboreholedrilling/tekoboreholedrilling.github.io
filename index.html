Category 01

Borehole Drilling Services

Our core offering — from the first geophysical survey through drilling, casing, development, and pump testing. Every borehole is backed by science, executed with precision, and fully documented for regulatory compliance.

01
🔬
Hydrogeological Survey
The essential first step — before any drilling begins

We conduct scientific assessment of underground water resources using proven geophysical methods to identify the optimal drilling location, estimate depth, and predict yield — dramatically reducing the risk of a dry or low-yield borehole.

Vertical Electrical Sounding (VES) geophysical testing
Satellite and GIS lineament analysis
Geological field mapping and rock outcrop analysis
Aquifer depth and yield estimation report
Recommended drilling location with GPS coordinates
Enquire Now → ✓ Included in all projects
02
⚙️
Borehole Drilling
DTH technology · domestic, commercial & industrial

Using advanced DTH (Down-The-Hole) drilling rigs — the most effective technology for Kenya's hard basement rock formations — we drill boreholes for all use cases, on time, on budget, and to engineering specification.

DTH hammer drilling rigs capable of 300m+ depth
Suitable for all Kenyan geology types
Real-time drilling log and formation records
Airflush borehole development on completion
Step-drawdown pump test and yield report
Enquire Now → ⏱ 3–7 days on-site
03
🏗️
Well Casing & Development
Protecting your investment for decades

Proper casing is the backbone of a long-lasting borehole. We install steel or PVC casing to prevent borehole collapse, protect against surface water contamination, and ensure structural integrity through Kenya's diverse soil and rock conditions.

Mild steel and uPVC casing options
Centraliser installation for uniform cement placement
Stainless steel wire-wound or slotted PVC screens
Gravel pack placement for aquifer protection
Concrete sanitary seal around wellhead
Enquire Now → 🛡 25–50yr lifespan
04
🔧
Borehole Rehabilitation
Restore yield · extend lifespan · avoid replacement

An underperforming or failed borehole doesn't always need to be replaced. Our rehabilitation service restores boreholes to full productivity through a range of proven techniques — often at a fraction of new drilling costs.

CCTV downhole camera inspection and diagnosis
High-pressure jetting and surging
Chemical acid treatment for biofouling
Casing repair and headworks reconstruction
Post-rehab pump test and performance comparison
Enquire Now → 💡 Often 60% cheaper than re-drilling

A standard residential borehole typically takes 3–7 days of on-site work from rig mobilisation to pump commissioning. Including the initial survey, permitting, and mobilisation, allow 2–4 weeks from first contact to water flowing. We provide a project timeline upfront.
Yes — all boreholes in Kenya require a WRMA Water Abstraction Permit and NEMA Environmental Clearance. Teko Engineering handles all permit applications on your behalf as standard practice. You do not need to engage the authorities directly.
Depth depends on local geology and the water table. In Kenya, productive boreholes range from 40m in coastal sedimentary zones to 200m+ in hard basement rock areas. Our hydrogeological survey determines the optimal depth for your specific site before any drilling begins.
Borehole water quality varies by location and geology. We include a basic quality report with every completed project. If treatment is required — for elevated fluoride, nitrates, or bacteria — we can supply and install appropriate filtration, UV, or reverse osmosis systems.
